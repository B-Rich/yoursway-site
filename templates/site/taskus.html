1. Type anything.

Taskus will save whatever text you type in. You can enter your ideas, tasks and drafts, and Taskus will instantly save every precious letter you add.

2. “Project:” and “- task”.

Use these two very simple conventions, and Taskus will recognize your projects and individual tasks.

3. You can add “@tags” or even “@tags(with additional info)”.

Taskus will autocomplete the tags you type, so tagging is very easy and fast.

Specify a context for your tasks, like @phone, @shop, @work or @computer. Choose the tasks you will do @today. Designate @urgent tasks, or mark tasks @due(tomorrow). Specify that you've @spent(2h) on something.

4. Tag done tasks with “@done”.

Just hit Command-D (or Control-D on Windows) to add or remove @done.

5. Synchronize with Basecamp. (Trac, Lighthouse and more to come.)

Add the following magic line below your project header, and the tasks from Basecamp will be added to the project automatically: “Sync with http://youraccount.seework.com/, project ‘My Task’, user ‘andreyvit’, todo list ‘Things to do’”. To publish your newly created tasks to Basecamp, tag them with @basecamp. {% endblock %}
