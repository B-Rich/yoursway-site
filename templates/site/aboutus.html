Andrey Tarantsov

I’m the CTO and the lead architect of our company. 15 years have passed since I was a proud DOS user, and I’ve been crazy about computer programming for all this time.

My experience ranges from GW-BASIC, through Assembler and standards-based C++ to Python, Ruby and Java. Among the languages I’ve never really used I know and appreciate Erlang, Haskell and Smalltalk, and I love Ada, which is sharing the label of my all-time favourite language with Ruby. I like writing beautiful code, and I believe that every language has its own unique style which makes the definition of “beautiful” very different for each of them.

I’ve lead and failed my first multiperson project at the age of 16, and I’ve learned a lot about managing software development since then. It was around the same time when I got to know that my favourite part of programming is called “object-oriented analysis and design”.

I switched to Mac in 2006 and never-ever-ever looked back.

Currently I’m mostly focused on Java and particularly on Eclipse world, though occasionally I get involved in Python and Ruby projects or have to apply my Win32 skills to make some crazy JNIs. I’ve contributed the initial Ruby type inference code to the Eclipse Dynamic Languages Toolkit and have then demoed it at EclipseCon 2007. I left the DLTK team afterwards and co-founded a company which aims at creating a new generation of IDEs and productivity tools for developers.

Leontiy Deriglazov

I am a software developer at YourSway LLC. Computer programming has become my passion since I’ve got my first PC in 1999. I am keen on tricky problems about programming and especially those that are practical. I like finding graceful solutions to them. The belief that they are useful is what moves me forward. Thus my general approach to software development consists in that I try doing things as easy as possible while preserving their practical value.

My professional interests now lie in the area of static code analysis and creation of related developer tools. I took part in development of Eclipse dynamic languages toolkit (DLTK) in 2007 and, recently, have been engaged into development of YourSway SADR. Besides these major projects, I took part in development for POSIX-based systems, enjoyed C++ and Python languages and recently I’ve had a lucky opportunity to program with Objective-C and Cocoa.

You might have already thought ‘What a geek is that guy!’. But programming is not all my life is about — I’m also fond sport. Workouts at a gym and swimming sessions make up my leisure time. I like keeping my shape and recently have brought this passion to our developer team. I believe it will contribute a lot to our team spirit.
