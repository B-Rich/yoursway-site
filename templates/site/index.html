Users

— is whom we care about, above all else. The purpose of every application is to make someone happier. To let them do things they couldn’t do before. To take away the chores and let them care about the fun stuff.
How do we care about the users of our software?

We take user interaction design seriously. We study use cases, do usability testing and apply the theory of usability. We recognize that users are most productive in flow state, and work hard to keep it.

We consider the impact on end users when making design decisions. We carefully design error handling strategy. We make sure our software does not break when users run out of disk space, suffer power outages, disconnect removable devices and move their laptops around.

Challenge

— is what drives us, and what we are looking for. We love to invent new concepts, scale to thousands of users, refactor huge applications, meet impossibly tight deadlines and solve problems that frighten everyone else.
Why do we think that every project is challenging?

Every project is challenging because every project benefits from us doing our best. Most need the best user experience possible and a well-designed, maintainable code. Some need a prototype out fast. Meeting the unique needs of every customer is never easy, and so that’s what we love to do.

Brain

— is an indispensable ingredient of good software. We often say we do “brain-driven development”. Our team consists of the smartest people we could get.
Why do we think that good software cannot be made by average developers?

Smart people make smart decisions. It only takes one lousy component to make the whole application unstable, and it takes one louse screen to break the user’s flow state. So we need to get lots of big and small decisions right, we need to not overlook thousands of corner cases and possible error conditions.
